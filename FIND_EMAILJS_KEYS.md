# How to Find Your EmailJS Keys

## Finding Your Public Key (User ID)

### **Step-by-Step:**

1. **Login to EmailJS**
   - Go to https://www.emailjs.com/
   - Login to your account

2. **Go to Account Settings**
   - Click your **profile icon/name** (top right corner)
   - OR
   - Click **"Account"** in the top menu

3. **Click "General"**
   - In the left sidebar, click **"General"**

4. **Find "Public Key"**
   - Scroll down to find **"Public Key"** section
   - It's also called **"User ID"**
   - It looks like: `user_xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx` or just a long string of letters/numbers

5. **Copy the Key**
   - Click the **copy icon** next to it
   - Or select and copy manually

## Visual Guide:

```

```

## All Three Keys You Need:

### **1. Public Key (User ID)**
- **Location:** Account → General → Public Key
- **Looks like:** `user_xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx` or `xxxxxxxxxxxxx`
- **Used for:** Initializing EmailJS

### **2. Service ID**
- **Location:** Email Services → Your Gmail Service
- **Looks like:** `service_xxxxxxxxx`
- **Used for:** Which email service to use

### **3. Template ID**
- **Location:** Email Templates → Your Template
- **Looks like:** `template_xxxxxxxxx`
- **Used for:** Which email template to use

## Quick Checklist:

- [ ] Public Key: Account → General
- [ ] Service ID: Email Services → Gmail Service
- [ ] Template ID: Email Templates → Password Reset Template

## If You Can't Find It:

1. Make sure you're logged in
2. Check the top right corner for your profile
3. Look for "Account" or "Settings"
4. The Public Key is always in "General" section
